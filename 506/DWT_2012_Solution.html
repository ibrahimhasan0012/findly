Complete Summary & Detailed Step-by-Step Solution

1Executive Summary

Original Title: Natural convection from a vertical plate embedded in a stratified medium with uniform heat source
Authors: Saha et al. (2012)
Core Question: How do Stratification (S) and Heat Generation (λ) affect convective flow?

The Core Concepts

  • The Setup: A hot vertical plate sits in a fluid. Normally, hot fluid rises (buoyancy).
  • The Twist (Stratification): The surrounding fluid isn't one temperature. It gets hotter as you go up (\( T_\infty = T_0 + Ax \)). This opposes buoyancy because the temperature difference (\( T_w - T_\infty \)) decreases as you go up!
  • The Second Twist (Heat Source): There is internal heat generation modeled as \( Q(T - T_\infty) \).

2The Exact Problem Statement (Page 2)

"Consider a steady two-dimensional viscous incompressible fluid on a vertical plate embedded in a stratified medium with uniform heat source."

Governing Equations (Eqs 2-4)

Continuity: \[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} = 0 \]
Momentum: \[ u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = \nu \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} + g\beta(T - T_{\infty}) \]
Energy: \[ u \frac{\partial T}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial T}{\partial y} = \alpha \frac{\partial^2 T}{\partial y^2} + \frac{Q}{\rho C_p}(T - T_{\infty}) \]

3Transformations (Page 3)

We convert PDEs to ODEs using similarity variables.

\( \psi = x^{3/4} f(x,\eta), \quad \eta = x^{-1/4}y \)

4Detailed Momentum Derivation

Substituting variables into the Momentum Equation.

Step B1: Calculate \( \partial u / \partial y \)
Using chain rule: \( \partial/\partial y = x^{-1/4} \partial/\partial \eta \)
\[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = x^{-1/4} \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta} (x^{1/2}f') = x^{1/4} f'' \]
Step B2: Calculate \( \partial^2 u / \partial y^2 \)
\[ \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = x^{-1/4} \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta} (x^{1/4}f'') = f''' \]

Calculus Deep Dive: \( \partial u / \partial x \)

This is the tricky term. We calculate \( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x^{1/2}f') \) using Product + Chain Rules.

1. Product Rule: Let \( A=x^{1/2} \), \( B=f' \)
\( \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}f' + x^{1/2}\frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} \)

2. Chain Rule for \( f'(x, \eta) \):
\( \frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial f'}{\partial \eta}\frac{\partial \eta}{\partial x} \)
Since \( \frac{\partial \eta}{\partial x} = -\frac{1}{4}x^{-1}\eta \), we have: \( \frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} - \frac{1}{4}x^{-1}\eta f'' \)

3. Combine:
\( \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}f' + x^{1/2}\frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} - \frac{1}{4}x^{-1/2}\eta f'' \)

Final Assembly (Cancellation):
When we combine \( u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \), the terms \( \pm \frac{1}{4}\eta f' f'' \) cancel out perfectly.
Momentum Equation (Eq 12):
\[ f''' + \frac{3}{4}ff'' - \frac{1}{2}(f')^2 + \theta = x\left(f'\frac{\partial f'}{\partial x} - f''\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right) \]

5Detailed Energy Derivation

Transforming the Energy Equation: \( u \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial y} = \frac{1}{Pr} \frac{\partial^2 \theta}{\partial y^2} + \lambda\theta \)

Step C1 & C2: Y-derivatives
\( \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial y} = x^{-1/4} \theta' \)
\( \frac{\partial^2 \theta}{\partial y^2} = x^{-1/2} \theta'' \)
Step C3: X-derivative (Chain Rule)
\( \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial x} - \frac{1}{4}x^{-1}\eta \theta' \)
Step C4: Cancellation Magic
Just like Momentum, the cross terms involving \( \eta \theta' \) cancel out when \( u \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial x} \) and \( v \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial y} \) are added together.
Energy Equation (Eq 13):
\[ \frac{1}{Pr}\theta'' + \frac{3}{4}f\theta' + \lambda\theta = x\left(f'\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial x} - \theta'\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right) \]

6Final Results (Page 6)

"The important physical quantities are... the skin friction and heat transfer rate."
Skin Friction Coefficient:
\[ \frac{1}{2} Gr_x^{1/4} C_f = f''(x,0) \]
Nusselt Number (Heat Transfer):
\[ \frac{Nu_x}{Gr_x^{1/4}} = -\theta'(x,0) \]
