Boundary layer flow
Laminar flow boundary layer equations was discovered by Ludwig Prandtl (1875-4953): 
In fluid dynamics, the boundary layer is a thin region adjacent to a solid surface where 
the effects of viscosity are significant. In laminar flow, the fluid moves in parallel layers 
with minimal mixing between them. The study of laminar 2D convective boundary layer 
flow is essential for applications in engineering and environmental science, where 
understanding heat and mass transfer is critical.
The behavior of laminar 2D convective boundary 
layer flow can be described by the simplified 
Navier-Stokes and energy  equations, which 
govern the motion of fluid substances.
